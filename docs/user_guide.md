# 📊 Your Impact Dashboard — A Guide for Programme Teams

> **Who is this guide for?**
> This guide is for Programme Managers, Field Coordinators, M&E Officers, and anyone at your NGO who wants to understand what the dashboard shows and how to use it. **No technical knowledge is needed.**

---

## What Is This Dashboard?

Think of this dashboard as a **health check-up report for your programmes**.

Just like a doctor's report shows your blood pressure, heart rate, and sugar levels in one place, this dashboard shows your programme's health — how much you're spending, how many people you're reaching, and whether lives are actually changing.

It answers **four big questions** that your Executive Director, your Board, and your donors will ask:

1. **"How much does it cost to change one life?"**
2. **"Are our programmes on track?"**
3. **"Who are we reaching — and who are we missing?"**
4. **"Are people actually better off after our intervention?"**

---

## How to Open the Dashboard

1. Open your web browser (Chrome, Firefox, or Edge — any will work)
2. Go to the dashboard link shared by your IT team (it will look like `http://your-ngo-name.metabase.com/dashboard/1`)
3. Log in with the username and password provided to you
4. The dashboard loads automatically — you're ready to go!

> **Tip**: Bookmark this link so you can find it easily every Monday morning.

## Understanding the Four Filters (Top of the Page)

At the very top of the dashboard, you'll see **four dropdown menus**. These are your "lenses" — they let you zoom in on specific data.

### 🗓️ Fiscal Year
- **What it does**: Shows data for a specific financial year (April to March)
- **Example**: Select "FY2023-24" to see only last year's data
- **Default**: Shows the current/latest fiscal year
- **When to use**: When preparing annual reports or comparing year-over-year progress

### 📋 Programme
- **What it does**: Filters everything to show only one specific programme
- **Example**: Select "Pragati Education Programme" to see only that programme's numbers
- **Default**: Shows all programmes combined
- **When to use**: When you're reviewing a specific programme's performance in a team meeting

### 📍 Region / State
- **What it does**: Filters by geographic location
- **Example**: Select "Rajasthan" to see only data from your Rajasthan operations
- **Default**: Shows all states combined
- **When to use**: When a State Lead wants to see their region's performance

### 🏷️ Sector
- **What it does**: Filters by the type of work (Education, Healthcare, WASH, etc.)
- **Example**: Select "Healthcare" to see all health-related programmes together
- **Default**: Shows all sectors combined
- **When to use**: When a Sector Head wants a cross-programme view of their domain

> **Important**: These filters work together. If you select "FY2023-24" AND "Rajasthan", you'll see only Rajasthan data from FY2023-24. To reset, click the "×" next to each filter or click "Clear All".

## The Four KPI Cards (The Big Numbers)

Right below the filters, you'll see **four large number cards**. These are the most important numbers on the entire dashboard.

Each card has:
- **A big number** — the headline metric
- **A small coloured dot** — Green (good), Amber/Yellow (needs attention), Red (action needed)
- **A small trend note** — whether things are improving or declining

---

### Card 1: 💰 Cost per Impact

**What it shows**: How many rupees it costs to produce one unit of positive change.

**In simple terms**: If your programme is training women in tailoring, this number tells you: *"It costs ₹287 for one woman to complete the training and start earning."*

**How to read it**:

| You see this | It means |
|---|---|
| **₹200 or less** (Green dot) | Excellent — your programme is very cost-efficient |
| **₹200 – ₹500** (Yellow dot) | Okay — look for ways to reduce costs without cutting quality |
| **More than ₹500** (Red dot) | High — investigate why. Are there unnecessary expenses? |

**What to do if it's RED**:
- Check which expense category (Personnel? Travel? Materials?) is driving the cost up
- Compare with other programmes — is this programme inherently more expensive, or is there waste?
- Discuss with your Finance team in the next review meeting

**Why this matters for donors**: This is the first number impact investors look at. A lower cost-per-impact makes your programme more fundable.

---

### Card 2: 📈 Programme Achievement

**What it shows**: What percentage of your target has been achieved.

**In simple terms**: If your plan was to reach 500 women this quarter, and you reached 420, your achievement is **84%**.

**How to read it**:

| You see this | It means |
|---|---|
| **90% or above** (Green dot) | On track — great work! |
| **70% – 89%** (Yellow dot) | Slightly behind — you can still catch up with extra effort |
| **Below 70%** (Red dot) | Significantly behind — needs immediate attention |

**What to do if it's YELLOW or RED**:
- Look at the **trend line chart** below this card — is the trend improving or getting worse?
- Talk to your Field Coordinators — what ground-level challenges are they facing?
- Consider: Was the target realistic? Sometimes targets set during proposal writing are too ambitious

> **Understanding the trend arrow**: An upward arrow (↑) next to the number means you're doing better than last quarter. A downward arrow (↓) means performance dipped. This is normal — what matters is the overall direction across 3-4 quarters.

---

### Card 3: 👥 Beneficiaries Reached

**What it shows**: How many individuals have actually received at least one service from your NGO.

**In simple terms**: Out of everyone registered in your database, this is the number who **actually showed up and received something** — a training, a health check-up, a counselling session, or any other service.

**Why "registered" is different from "reached"**:

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Registered** | People who signed up or were enrolled in your records |
| **Reached (Active)** | People who actually came and received a service |

The number in parentheses (e.g., "of 2,000 registered — 72% activation") tells you what percentage of registered people actually participated. If this percentage is low, it means people are signing up but not showing up.

**What to do if the activation rate is low**:
- Are beneficiaries dropping out? Why? (distance, timing, relevance?)
- Are registrations being inflated by field teams? (data quality issue)
- Is the programme design not meeting community needs?

---

### Card 4: ✅ Showed Improvement

**What it shows**: Of the people who took **both** a "before" test and an "after" test, what percentage actually improved.

**In simple terms**: Before your literacy programme, Anita scored 40 out of 100 on a reading test. After six months in the programme, she scored 75. Anita **improved**. This card tells you: *"68% of all tested beneficiaries showed improvement, like Anita."*

**How to read it**:

| You see this | It means |
|---|---|
| **70% or more improved** (Green dot) | Strong evidence your intervention is working |
| **50% – 69% improved** (Yellow dot) | Mixed results — investigate which groups aren't improving |
| **Below 50% improved** (Red dot) | Concerning — the intervention may need redesign |

**What to do if it's RED**:
- Look at the **Outcome Effectiveness table** in the lower section — which programmes have the weakest improvement?
- Check the "Baseline Score" column — if people already had high scores before, there's less room to improve (this is called a "ceiling effect")
- Talk to your M&E Officer about the assessment quality — are the pre-tests and post-tests measuring the right things?

## The Charts (Middle Section)

### Cost per Impact Bar Chart (Left Side)

**What it shows**: A horizontal bar for each programme, showing how cost-efficient it is.

- **Shorter bars** = more efficient (less money per unit of impact)
- **Longer bars** = less efficient (more money per unit of impact)
- Bars are coloured by **sector** (Education = blue, Healthcare = green, etc.)
- The **vertical dotted line** shows the average across all programmes

**How to use it**: Look for programmes whose bar extends far beyond the average line — these may need a cost review.

**Clicking a bar**: When you click on a programme's bar, the detail table at the bottom updates to show only that programme's data.

---

### Quarterly Trend Line (Right Side)

**What it shows**: How each programme's achievement percentage has changed quarter by quarter.

- **Lines going up** = programme is improving
- **Lines going down** = programme is declining
- **Flat lines** = no change (could be good or concerning, depending on where the line sits)
- The **dashed lines** at 90% and 70% show the Green and Amber thresholds

**How to use it**: Don't worry about one bad quarter — look at the overall direction across 3-4 quarters. If a line is consistently below the 70% dashed line, that programme needs attention.

---

### Demographic Breakdown (Lower Left)

**What it shows**: Who your beneficiaries are, broken down by:
- **Gender** (Male / Female / Non-Binary)
- **Income** (BPL / Low Income / Middle Income)
- **Location** (Urban / Rural / Tribal)
- **Disability** (Person with Disability / Not recorded)

**Why this matters**: Donors and government agencies increasingly want to see that your programmes are **equitable** — that you're not accidentally excluding vulnerable groups.

**What to look for**:
- Is the gender split matching your target? (e.g., a women's empowerment programme should show 80%+ female)
- Are you reaching enough BPL (Below Poverty Line) families?
- Are tribal and rural areas getting fair representation?

---

### Outcome Effectiveness Table (Lower Right)

**What it shows**: A programme-by-programme breakdown of test score improvements.

| Column | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Baseline Score** | Average score BEFORE the intervention |
| **Endline Score** | Average score AFTER the intervention |
| **Improvement** | How much the score went up (in percentage points) |
| **% Improved** | What fraction of people got better |

**Colour coding**: Cells are coloured from **red** (no improvement) through **yellow** (some improvement) to **green** (strong improvement).

## Frequently Asked Questions

### "The numbers look wrong. What should I do?"
First, check your filters — you may have accidentally filtered to one region or programme. Click "Clear All" in the filter bar. If the numbers still look wrong, contact your M&E Officer or Data team.

### "Can I download the data?"
Yes! Scroll to the bottom detail table. Most BI tools have a "Download" or "Export" button that lets you save the data as a CSV or Excel file.

### "How often is the data updated?"
This depends on your NGO's data pipeline. Ask your IT team whether data refreshes daily, weekly, or monthly. The "last updated" timestamp is usually shown in the dashboard header.

### "I want to see data for a specific village/block. Can I?"
The dashboard filters go down to the **State** and **District** level. If you need village-level data, use the detail table at the bottom and search for the beneficiary or intervention you're looking for.

### "What does 'activation rate' mean?"
It's the percentage of registered beneficiaries who actually received at least one service. Think of it as the difference between "people we signed up" and "people we actually helped." A 100% activation rate means everyone who registered also received services.

### "A programme shows RED. Should I be worried?"
RED means performance is below 70% of the target. It doesn't mean the programme is failing — it means it needs attention. Possible reasons:
- The target was set too high during proposal writing
- There were ground-level challenges (monsoon, COVID, political disruptions)
- The programme design needs adjustment
- There's a data collection gap (services delivered but not recorded)

Discuss RED items in your next programme review meeting. The dashboard gives you the data — your team provides the context.
